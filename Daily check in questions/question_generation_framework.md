## QUESTION TEMPLATES BY TYPE

### Scale Questions (1-10) Templates:

```
- "On a scale of 1-10, how [emotion] do you feel about [financial topic]?"
- "Rate your [attribute] when it comes to [financial situation]"
- "How confident are you about [financial action]?" (1=not confident, 10=very confident)
- "To what extent do you feel [emotion] about [money topic]?" (1=not at all, 10=extremely)
```

Examples to generate:
- Stress about different expense categories
- Confidence in different financial tasks
- Satisfaction with different financial metrics
- Control over various money situations

### Multiple Choice Templates:

```
- "What's your biggest [category] right now?"
- "When do you tend to [behavior]?"
- "Which of these best describes your [situation]?"
- "How often do you [action]?"
- "What prevents you from [goal]?"
```

Options should be:
- Comprehensive (cover most scenarios)
- Non-judgmental
- Include "Other" or "None" option
- 5-8 choices typically

### Open Text Templates:

```
- "Describe a time when [financial situation]..."
- "What does [financial concept] mean to you?"
- "If you could [hypothetical], what would you do?"
- "What's your earliest memory of [money topic]?"
- "Complete this sentence: Money is..."
```

Good for:
- Deeper exploration
- Personal stories
- Future visioning
- Values clarification

### Yes/No Templates:

```
- "Do you [behavior]?"
- "Have you ever [experience]?"
- "Are you currently [situation]?"
- "Would you [hypothetical action]?"
```

Use when:
- Need binary data
- Building user profile
- Qualifying for follow-up questions

## VARIATION TECHNIQUES

### 1. Time-Based Variations
Same question, different timeframes:
- "How stressed are you about money **right now**?"
- "How stressed **were you** about money **this morning**?"
- "How stressed do you **expect to be** about money **this week**?"

### 2. Comparison Variations
- "Compared to **last month**, how do you feel?"
- "Compared to **a year ago**, how confident are you?"
- "Compared to **your peers**, how do you rate your situation?"

### 3. Intensity Variations
Same concept, different intensity words:
- Worried → Anxious → Stressed → Panicked
- Confident → Comfortable → Secure → Certain
- Interested → Motivated → Excited → Passionate

### 4. Specificity Variations
General → Specific:
- "How do you feel about spending?" 
- "How do you feel about spending on food?"
- "How do you feel about spending on takeout?"
- "How do you feel about spending on DoorDash?"

### 5. Context Variations
Same question in different contexts:
- "Do you track your spending?" (general)
- "Do you track your spending when traveling?"
- "Do you track your spending during holidays?"
- "Do you track your spending when stressed?"

## QUALITY CHECKLIST FOR NEW QUESTIONS

Before adding a question, verify:

✓ **Clear & Concise**: Can user understand in 3 seconds?
✓ **Non-Judgmental**: No shame-inducing language
✓ **Actionable Insights**: Will answer help us help them?
✓ **Unique**: Not too similar to existing questions
✓ **Appropriate Tags**: Properly categorized for routing
✓ **Life Stage Relevant**: Tagged for right audience
✓ **Answer Type Fits**: Scale/choice/text makes sense

## BULK GENERATION WORKFLOW

### Step 1: Pick a Category
Choose from the 23 categories listed above

### Step 2: Create Subcategory Outline
Break category into 5-10 subcategories

### Step 3: Apply Templates
For each subcategory, create questions using:
- 40% multiple choice
- 30% scale (1-10)
- 20% yes/no
- 10% open text

### Step 4: Add Variations
For high-value questions, create 3-5 variations using techniques above

### Step 5: Quality Check
Review against checklist, ensure no duplicates

### Step 6: Add to Database
Include proper metadata: ID, category, tags, life_stages

## EXAMPLE: Generating 50 Variations on "Stress About Rent"

1. "On a scale of 1-10, how stressed are you about paying rent this month?"
2. "How confident are you that you can make rent on time?"
3. "What's your biggest worry about rent?"

4. "How many days before rent is due do you start feeling anxious?"
5. "Do you lose sleep over rent payments?"
6. "Have you ever been late on rent?"
7. "What percentage of your income goes to rent?"
8. "Does your rent stress interfere with other spending decisions?"
9. "How would you feel if rent was $200 less per month?"
10. "Have you considered moving to reduce rent?"
... continue to 50
